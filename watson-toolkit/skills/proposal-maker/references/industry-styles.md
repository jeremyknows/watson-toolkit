# Industry Style Starting Points

These are starting points for visual direction, not prescriptions. Always override with:
1. The client's actual brand identity
2. Reference sites they mentioned
3. Your own judgment about what fits

The best proposals don't look like "an industry template." They look like they were designed for THIS client.

## By Industry

**Tech / SaaS** - Dark backgrounds, accent colors (cyan, purple, lime). Geometric sans-serif or monospace typography. Glass effects, gradients. But: the "dark SaaS landing page" is overdone. If the client's brand is light/clean, follow their brand.

**Food & Beverage** - Warm tones, product colors as accents. Rounded or humanist typography. Product imagery matters. The visual should make you hungry/thirsty.

**Health & Wellness** - Earth tones, natural greens, warm neutrals, lots of white space. Serif or humanist sans. Calming, not busy. Trust signals matter more than flash.

**Professional Services / B2B** - Navy, charcoal, or slate with gold or blue accents. Modern serif or clean sans. Data-forward: tables, comparisons, authority. Feels reliable.

**Creative / Design / Agency** - Bold colors, confident typography, asymmetric layouts. This is where you show off. The proposal should look like nothing the client has seen before.

**E-commerce / Retail** - Brand-color dominant, clear CTAs, product-centric. High contrast, social proof. Conversion-optimized feel.

**Finance / Fintech** - Dark + green/gold, or clean white + navy. Number-forward. Monospace for figures. Precision matters.

## When the Client Spans Categories

Match the TARGET AUDIENCE, not the product. A health food brand targeting B2B coffee shops should feel like Professional Services with Food & Beverage warmth, not pure wellness.

## When the Client Has a Strong Existing Brand

Follow it. Don't impose a different aesthetic. If their brand is flat/minimal with lots of white space, don't add gradients and glass. The proposal should feel like a natural extension of their world, elevated.
