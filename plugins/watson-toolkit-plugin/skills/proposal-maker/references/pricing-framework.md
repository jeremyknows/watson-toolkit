# Pricing Framework

## The Core Rule

**Pricing is research-driven, not formula-driven.** Don't guess. Find real benchmarks.

## Step 1: Research Benchmarks

Search for:
- "[industry] [engagement type] agency pricing"
- "[competitor agency name] pricing page"
- "[engagement type] cost [year]" (e.g., "fractional CMO cost 2026")
- Clutch.co, Agency Analytics, or industry reports for rate benchmarks

Find 3 real data points. These anchor your recommendation.

## Step 2: Size the Client

| Client signal | Price sensitivity | Approach |
|--------------|-------------------|----------|
| Pre-revenue / startup | High | Lower base, offer revenue share or equity |
| Small business ($100K-$1M rev) | Medium | Mid-range, emphasize ROI payback period |
| Growth stage ($1M-$10M rev) | Low-medium | Standard agency rates, value-based pricing |
| Enterprise ($10M+) | Low | Premium pricing, procurement-friendly terms |

If you don't know their revenue, estimate from: team size, office/location, existing marketing spend, industry norms.

## Step 3: Choose the Model

| Model | When | How to price |
|-------|------|-------------|
| One-time project | Clear scope, defined deliverables | Position between your benchmarks. Justify with ROI: "This costs $15K and generates $36K/year." |
| Monthly retainer | Ongoing work, relationship | 3-month minimum. Price where the monthly ROI exceeds the monthly cost by 3x+. |
| Fractional role | Deep integration | Research full-time salary for the role in the client's market. Price at 30-50% of that. |
| Revenue share | High confidence, aligned incentives | Lower base (50-70% of normal) + 5-15% of attributable revenue. Cap or sunset clause. |

## Step 4: Structure the Options

**Two options only.** Never three or more.

- **Option A (recommended):** The engagement you want to sell. Visually featured on the page. Priced where the ROI math makes it obvious.
- **Option B:** Smaller entry point. Clear upgrade path to Option A. For clients who need to build trust first.

Show recommended option first (anchoring). Make it visually distinct: glow, border, badge.

## Step 5: Sanity Check

Before finalizing:
- [ ] Can you justify this price with the benchmark data you found?
- [ ] Does the ROI math show at least 3x return?
- [ ] Is the price proportional to the client's business size?
- [ ] Are commitment terms included? (Duration, support window, what happens after)
- [ ] Is there a clear upgrade path from Option B to Option A?
- [ ] Would you be comfortable defending this price in a live conversation?

## Geography Adjustment

If the client is outside the US or in a different cost market:
- Research local agency pricing (search in local language if possible)
- Adjust for purchasing power, not just currency conversion
- Payment terms vary by country (Net 30 is US standard, Net 60-90 is common in EU/APAC)
- Some markets expect longer engagement commitments upfront
