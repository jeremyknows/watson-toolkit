# doc-coauthoring

A structured three-stage workflow for writing documentation collaboratively with an AI — from raw context all the way to a reader-tested final doc.

---

## What It Does

Guides you through co-authoring any structured document: technical specs, decision docs, PRDs, proposals, RFCs, design docs. The key insight is the third stage: testing the finished doc with a fresh Claude instance (no shared context) to catch blind spots before other humans read it.

---

## When to Use

- Writing documentation, proposals, or technical specs
- Any substantial writing task where you want the doc to work for readers who don't share your context
- When you keep explaining things in the doc that the doc itself doesn't explain

**Not for:** quick one-paragraph writeups or notes where structure isn't needed.

---

## The Three Stages

### Stage 1: Context Gathering
Close the gap between what you know and what the AI knows. You answer meta questions: document type, audience, desired impact, format constraints, anything else relevant. The AI asks clarifying questions before writing anything.

### Stage 2: Refinement & Structure
Build each section iteratively. Brainstorm → draft → edit. Work section by section until the doc holds together.

### Stage 3: Reader Testing
Paste the finished doc into a fresh Claude conversation (no prior context). Ask it to explain the doc back to you. Gaps in the explanation reveal blind spots in the writing — places where you assumed shared context that isn't actually on the page. Fix those before sharing with anyone.

---

## How to Trigger

Mention writing documentation, creating a proposal, drafting a spec, or starting a substantial writing task. The skill will offer the structured workflow or let you work freeform — your choice.

---

## Output

A complete document, refined through iteration and validated by reader testing.
